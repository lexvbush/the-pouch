01 — Overview
What we're building and why we need you
We are launching a new women's carry brand built around a single hero product — a small, soft, organized pocket pouch designed to hold a woman's everyday essentials. The brand has a strong strategic and written foundation. What we need now is a designer to bring it to life visually.
The brand name is currently in development. You will be designing the identity system before the final name is locked — logo concepts will be done in placeholder form and finalized once the name is confirmed. This is standard and we will move quickly on naming once direction is established.
"We need a designer who can move fast, take direction, and collaborate openly — not someone who disappears and returns with one take-it-or-leave-it concept."
This is a paid engagement. We are collecting quotes and will move forward with the designer whose work, communication style, and timeline best fits what we're building.
02 — The Product
One product. Every woman needs it.
The product is a small, soft, shapeless exterior pouch made from micro-velvet. It is engineered for women — with a fully organized interior featuring dedicated zones for lipstick, chapstick, jewelry, business cards, AirPods, small keys, hair ties, and mints. It moves from purse to pocket to standalone carry. The mission is to create a more functional existence for women.
Product identity in one line
"Carry your woman with you."
Physical product details relevant to design
  • Exterior: micro-velvet, soft and tactile — the material itself is the luxury signal
  • Two launch colorways, both monochrome: Deep Ink (near-black) and Stone at Dawn (warm off-white)
  • No zippers — closures are magnetic snap and elastic
  • Small brand mark blind-embossed on exterior center-bottom
  • Approximately 4.5" × 3.5" — pocket-sized, compresses flat when empty
  • Price range: $38–$62 retail — accessible luxury positioning
03 — Brand World & Aesthetic Direction
The visual world already exists — we need you to formalize it
The brand's visual direction has been developed through a Pinterest board and written brand guidelines. The world is painterly, lush, and alive — Mediterranean coastlines, illustrated landscapes in the style of Studio Ghibli, Chinese ink-wash mountains, overgrown canal cities, tropical interiors. Warm, saturated, abundant. Not minimal, not corporate, not cold.
Six words that define the brand
Painterly Abundant Wandering Contemplative Handmade Luminous

These colors are directional, not locked. The designer is encouraged to refine, expand, or evolve the palette as part of the identity work — with the understanding that vibrant green is the primary accent color and amber replaces any orange/terracotta usage.
Logo direction
The logo mark concept is a swirl with an upper and lower half — reminiscent of the laws of nature. Ethereal, feminine, and powerful. Think nautilus, golden ratio, dual opposing forces. Fine-line illustrative quality. The wordmark pairs with this mark in an italic serif. The brand name is TBD — logo concepts should be done with a placeholder name or mark-only initially.
Typography direction
  • Display / headline: an italic serif with elegance and weight — Playfair Display is the current placeholder
  • Body: a clean, modern light-weight sans — warm, never corporate
  • The combination should feel like an elevated editorial magazine, not a tech product
Photography & imagery tone
  • The product lives in the world — on stone terraces, cobblestone alleys, beside bay windows, in botanical interiors
  • Natural light always. Golden hour preferred for the dark colorway. Open midday light for the pale colorway
  • Never: white seamless, generic flat-lay, stock imagery
  • Illustrated scenes are part of the brand identity — think Ghibli-esque moments featuring the product in the brand world. The designer may be asked to concept these or art-direct them separately
04 — Deliverables
What we need designed and delivered
All deliverables are digital. Files should be delivered in editable source format (Figma preferred, Adobe XD or Illustrator accepted) plus exported PNGs and SVGs at appropriate resolutions. Print-ready files are not required at this stage.
01
Logo system
Primary logo mark (the swirl concept) + wordmark combination. Secondary mark (sprig botanical seal for emboss use). Mark-only version. Wordmark-only version. All in black, white, and primary brand green. Usage guidelines for each variant.
core deliverable
02
Brand identity system
Finalized color palette with hex codes and usage rules. Typography pairing with hierarchy (H1–H3, body, caption, label). Spacing and layout principles. Do/don't usage examples. Delivered as a brand guidelines document (Figma or PDF).
core deliverable
03
Packaging design concepts
Botanical-illustrated tissue paper wrap (one scene, digital mockup). Hang tag (front and back). Insert card / postcard (front: illustrated scene, back: one-line copy). Glassine sleeve label. All as digital mockups — no print files required at this stage.
priority deliverable
04
Product photography mood board & shot list
A curated visual mood board (12–16 reference images) showing the exact aesthetic, lighting, surfaces, and settings for product photography. A written shot list covering hero shots, colorway-specific shots, lifestyle shots, and detail shots. This gives the photographer everything they need.
priority deliverable
05
Social media templates
Instagram feed post template (1:1 and 4:5). Instagram Stories template. One TikTok cover / thumbnail template. Templates should be editable in Figma and follow the brand system. Not campaign-specific — these are the blank canvas brand templates.
standard deliverable
06
Website visual direction
Homepage design mockup (desktop + mobile). Product page design mockup (desktop + mobile). This is visual design only — not development. We will handle the Shopify build using the design files. Delivered in Figma with organized, named layers.
standard deliverable
07
Product illustration / tech pack (clean final)
A clean, final-quality technical illustration of the product — front, interior, and side views. Suitable for use in the business brief, wholesale deck, and production documents. Should clearly show all five interior compartments. Line art style preferred.
bonus / optional

06 — Project Terms
How this engagement works
Non-negotiable requirements
Please only quote if you agree to all of the following:

  • You are available for feedback calls or async check-ins during the project window. Disappearing after kickoff is not acceptable.
  • All work is original. No stock illustration, no AI-generated imagery submitted as design work.
  • You deliver working, organized Figma files — not flattened exports only. We need to be able to edit templates ourselves after handoff.
  • You agree that all final approved assets transfer fully to us upon final payment. No licensing terms, no usage restrictions.
  • You will not use our brand, product, or identity work in your portfolio without explicit written permission from us.
Timeline1–2 weeks from project kickoff. If you cannot meet this, please state your realistic timeline in your quote.

Revisions2–3 rounds included per deliverable. Rounds beyond that are quoted separately.
Payments50% upfront to begin, 50% on final delivery and approval. Payment via bank transfer or PayPal.
Working filesFigma preferred. All layers named, organized, and editable. Exports provided as PNG (transparent background) and SVG for all logo variants.
Name TBDThe brand name is not yet finalized. Logo concepts begin with a placeholder. Once the name is confirmed, one round of wordmark finalization is included at no additional cost.
Digital onlyAll deliverables are digital. Print-ready files, physical samples, and production-ready packaging artwork are not in scope for this engagement.
