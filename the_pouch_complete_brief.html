01 — Purpose & Mission
Built to create a more functional existence for women
The Pouch is a small, soft, shapeless carry object engineered specifically for women. Not a bag. Not a wallet. Not a clutch. Something entirely new — a single organized object that moves with her: from purse to jacket pocket to her hand, worn alone or tucked inside anything.
Every woman knows the feeling: switching bags and losing twenty minutes. Reaching into a purse for a lipstick and finding everything except it. Wearing an outfit with no pockets and having nowhere to put the three things that matter. The Pouch solves all of it in one object.
"Carry your woman with you."
The Pouch · Product & Brand Mission
  • Mission: To create a more functional existence for women through one beautifully designed, highly organized carry object.
  • Vision: A world where women move through their day without friction — where everything they need is always exactly where they left it.
  • Values: Function first. Craft that shows. Designed by and for women. Beautiful without being precious.
02 — Product Design
Soft outside. Engineered inside.
The Pouch is intentionally shapeless on the exterior — soft, velvet-touch, compressible. But open it and everything has a home. Five dedicated interior zones, each designed for a specific essential a woman actually carries. The contrast between its formless exterior and its precisely organized interior is the product's defining character.

Materials & construction
Exterior
Velvet-touch soft fabric. Rich, durable, tactile. Not quilted nylon — the feel is closer to high-grade micro-velvet. Feels considered, not sporty.
Interior lining
Structured microfiber holds compartment shape. Jewelry vault in suede-touch lining — protects delicate pieces from scratching and tangling.
Closures
YKK smooth-pull main zipper. Secondary YKK zipper on jewelry vault. Card slide is open-top with stiff-back panel for clean card access.
Weight & size
Under 3oz empty. Compresses flat under 0.75". Expands to ~4.5" × 3.5" × 1.5" full. Fits any pocket without bulk or bulge.

04 — Social Messaging & Campaigns
Content that speaks directly to her
Every piece of content either deepens the brand world or directly solves a problem women recognize in their own lives. Platforms: Instagram (primary visual brand), TikTok (discovery and relatability), Pinterest (evergreen visual engine). Post with intention. Never chase trends.
Four campaign pillars
Campaign 01 — "The Switch"
The bag-switch moment on TikTok/Reels. One move with the Pouch vs. the usual chaos without it. Zero dialogue needed. Universally understood by every woman who has ever switched purses. High share rate.
Campaign 02 — "What's in yours?"
UGC-driven. Community photos of Pouch contents, unposed. Reposted with first name and city only. Builds social proof through honest specificity. Runs continuously.
Campaign 03 — "Made for this"
Short illustrated scenes of the Pouch in the brand world. A rooftop dinner. A stone terrace in the south of France. A dim hotel room. World-building, not product advertising.
Campaign 04 — "The five things"
What are your five non-negotiables? Women tag their everyday five. The Pouch holds all of them. Positions it as the object that makes the edit possible.

05 — Product Imagery Direction
Where The Pouch lives
Every image should feel like a still from a film set in the brand world. Warm, painted, alive. The Pouch is always the natural inhabitant of its setting — never placed artificially. Shot on location or set-dressed to feel like a real place in the brand's world.
Hero shots
  • The Pouch on a stone ledge above a turquoise bay. Morning light. Shot from above. A book or worn map beside it.
  • Open on a linen-covered table in a Mediterranean room. Botanical wallpaper behind. Contents visible — three items only. Natural light from one side.
  • In a woman's hand, mid-motion on a cobblestone street. Soft focus on the street behind. The velvet catches the afternoon light.
  • Flat lay on aged paper, pressed botanicals, or an illustrated map. Texture on texture — the velvet reads against the surface.
  • Tucked into a jacket breast pocket, barely visible above the edge. The whole product story in one frame.
By colorway
Deep Ink
Lower, warmer light. Evening or golden hour. Candlelight optional. The deep velvet absorbs and reflects simultaneously. Surfaces: aged wood, dark marble, worn leather.
Stone at Dawn
Midday, open air, dappled shade. The pale velvet feels cool and fresh. Surfaces: white stone, cool marble, salt-bleached wood. Light feels clean and honest.
What we never shoot
  • White seamless or flat-lay on white background.
  • Generic hands holding a product with no setting context.
  • Stock lifestyle imagery that could belong to any brand.
  • Perfectly staged scenes that look designed — it should always look found.
06 — Web Design Direction
A site that breathes
The website feels like walking into a room where one object is made very well — and the room itself is beautiful. Sparse text. Large, warm imagery. No popups, no countdown timers, no upsell carousels. The site is the brand extended.
Page architecture
Homepage
Full-bleed hero image — the Pouch in the world. One-line Playfair italic headline. Single CTA: "Shop the Pouch." No announcement bar. Scroll begins the story.
Product page
4–5 images per colorway. Colorway selector. Monogram add-on toggle. Collapsible: What's inside, Materials, Dimensions, Care, Shipping.
Our Why
Long-form essay on why this exists and for whom. Not bullet points — a piece of writing worth reading. The mission lives here.
The Journal
Occasional editorial posts — illustrated scenes, carry profiles, maker stories. Irregular cadence, high quality. SEO vehicle and brand depth.
UX & tech
  • Maximum 2-click checkout. No account required to purchase.
  • Cart as a drawer, not a page. Mobile-first layout throughout.
  • Page speed as a brand value — Lighthouse target: 90+.
  • Shopify + custom theme. Klaviyo for email. Gorgias for support.
  • Email: under 4 sends per month. Open rate target: 48%+. Welcome series: 3 emails — origin story, materials, community.
07 — Sales Method
Direct, deliberate, distributed
The Pouch sells primarily direct-to-consumer, with a curated wholesale strategy for partners who share the brand's world. We build depth, not volume. Amazon is not a channel for The Pouch.
Sales channels
DTC — primary
thepouch.com. Own the relationship. Full margin. Email list as core asset. Limited-quantity drops for new colorways — announced 72 hours in advance.
Wholesale — curated
Max 30 partners globally. Independent boutiques, apothecaries, hotel gift shops, concept stores. Selected for brand alignment. Min order: 12 units.
Pop-ups & markets
Quarterly in key cities. Designed as brand experiences — a cloth-covered table, both colorways, a botanical prop. Sells out. Creates community.
Corporate gifting
Min 24 units. Custom emboss. White-glove onboarding. Target: law firms, creative agencies, boutique hotels. Margin-positive.
Gift market
Positioned as the most considered gift for a woman. Mother's Day, birthdays, holiday. Campaign calendar aligned to gifting moments.

09 — Production Methods
Made with care. Built to last.
The velvet construction requires a specialist. The feel of the exterior is the first thing a woman touches — we do not compromise on it. Production is a direct expression of the brand.
Phase 1 — Launch (months 1–6)
Small-batch, quality-first production
300–500 units per colorway per SKU. Single vetted workshop with soft-goods velvet expertise. Full quality oversight. Failure rate target: under 2%. Higher COGS acceptable at launch — quality integrity is non-negotiable.
Phase 2 — Scale (year 1–2)
Certified ethical manufacturer
Transition to BSCI/SA8000-certified manufacturer with proven velvet expertise — Portugal, Turkey, or South Korea are strong velvet production regions. Reduce COGS by 25–35% while maintaining standards. Third-party quality audits at production and pre-shipment.
Phase 3 — Maturity
Sustainability & transparency
Full supply chain transparency on website. Explore OEKO-TEX certified or recycled-fiber velvet. Repair program: free for Signature tier, small fee for Classic. Positions the product as durable, not disposable.
Fulfillment
  • In-house for first 12 months — packaging assembly is too important to outsource.
  • Transition to 3PL (ShipBob or Shipmonk) at 400+ monthly orders.
  • Botanical-illustrated tissue, glassine sleeve, postcard insert, kraft mailer.
10 — Humanitarian Efforts & Brand Mission
The work beyond the product
The Pouch's mission — a more functional existence for women — extends beyond the product itself. Our humanitarian commitments focus on economic empowerment, skill-building, and opportunity for women. Built into the business from day one, not added later as an afterthought.
01
Vocational training partnership
For every 50 Pouches sold, The Pouch funds one day of soft-goods vocational training for women re-entering the workforce, displaced individuals, or formerly incarcerated women. Year one target: 300 training days funded. Partner with existing NGOs.
02
The Maker Fellowship
Annual program supporting 2–3 emerging women artisans from underrepresented backgrounds with $5,000 in materials, mentorship, and a limited production run with The Pouch. Fellows design a seasonal limited-edition variant. Full credit and compensation.
03
1% for women's economic programs
1% of annual revenue pledged to organizations focused on women's economic empowerment globally. Selection criteria and annual allocation published transparently on the website. Not a marketing line — a structural commitment.
04
Circular return program
Customers return worn Pouches for a $10–$15 credit toward a new one. Returned Pouches are repaired and donated to vocational programs or resold at a reduced price. Zero-waste ambition with real, trackable structure.
